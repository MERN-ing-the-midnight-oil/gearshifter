**BIKE SWAP APP OVERVIEW**

**Flow of Program**

1. Consignee goes to Shifting Gears website and clicks on a button that takes them to a website where they have to register, create a login and upload all the items they want to sell.   
   1. INFORMATION NEEDED:  
      1. Full Name (First and Last)  
      2. Phone Number  
      3. Email  
      4. Item Category (Accessory, Bike, Bike Part, Bike Rack, Clothes, Shoes, Other)  
      5. Item Description (suggested descriptors \- Color, make, model, size)  
      6. Item Price  
      7. Option to lower the price at xx time (Yes or No)  
      8. Amount they want to lower the price to at xx time   
         NOTE: xx time will only be decided in April/May, we can use 4pm as a starting point.   
      9. Option to donate if items don't sell (so that we know to put those in our donation pile)  
2. On the day of the Swap, the consignee brings their items and some form of identification so that the Shifting Gears Team (SG Team) can find their name and list of items.  
   1. Maybe the consignee brings a generated QR code which the ST Team can scan to bring up the consignees list of items.   
   2. If someone has not registered beforehand, they can download an app, or go to the SG website, click the register button and register on their phone at the venue, or use a SG tablet to complete the registration of their items. Once they have done this, they can head over to get their labels printed for their items with an SG Team Member.   
3. From this personalized list, the SG Team prints a label for each item, and helps the consignee attach it to each item. (We would like to have 6 stations doing this at the same time.)  
   1. INFORMATION NEEDED ON THE LABEL:  
      1. Unique item number  
      2. Name of person  
      3. Name of item  
      4. Category  
      5. Description  
      6. Size  
      7. Price  
      8. Reduced price at XX time  
      9. Item to be donated after the Swap if not sold  
         DO NOT INCLUDE ANY PERSONAL CONTACT INFO \- no phone number or email\!  
4. The item with the label gets placed on the 'shelves' ready for sale and the consignee can go home.   
5. During the actual 'Shop the Swap, when an item is purchased, a SG Team member scans the label and takes payment through our Square Up POS. This will trigger 2 things:  
   1. The SG inventory list gets updated with the sold price.  
      1. If this item is sold after xx time, and the consignee opted to lower the price, then the sold price that gets updated is the lower price chosen by the consignee.   
   2. The consignee gets a notification that their item has sold and how much it was sold for.    
6. When 'Shop the Swap' closes, 2 things happen:  
   1. SG gets a list of consignees with each item sold, each item not sold and the amount SG owes the consignee for their sold items.   
   2. The consignee gets their own list of all the items that sold and how much they are owed by SG, and a list of items that did not sell.   
7. The consignee comes to the 'Pick Up' with their lists and identification. (Again, potentially a QR code that the SG Team scans to find the list of sold and unsold items for that particular individual.)  
   1. An SG Team member finds their name on the list, and the amount owed to the consignee and writes a check for that amount. The Consignee signs the list to say they have received the check and the ST team member types in the check number.   
   2. If the consignee has any items that have not sold, they take that list to another SG Team member who will help them find their items and check that they are only taking their items.   
8. At the end of the event, once checks and items have been picked up by consignees, ideally Shifting Gears will have a list of donated items still in inventory that we can:  
   1. Take to the dump or Goodwill/Value Village. We can scan the item and delete it from our inventory.  
   2. Any items that we want to keep for the following year stay in our inventory and we can reuse the label the following year. The items remaining that go into storage should match the inventory list of items on the app.
